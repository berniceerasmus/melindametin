Phase 2 · Lesson 15

Present Continuous for Future 📅

Learn how to talk about fixed future arrangements using the present continuous.

Warm-Up

🔥 What's in your diary? (3 minutes)

Think about your schedule. What is already arranged for this week or month?

  • Do you have any training sessions this week?
  • Are you meeting anyone special soon?
  • Do you have any trips or events coming up?

Share with your teacher. Notice how we describe arrangements that are already confirmed!

Presentation

📌 Present Continuous for Fixed Future Arrangements

FormStructureExample
Affirmativeam/is/are + verb-ingI am meeting my coach tomorrow.
Negativeam/is/are + not + verb-ingShe isn't coming to the party.
QuestionAm/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?Are you playing on Saturday?
Use it when the arrangement is already confirmed:

✅ We are having dinner with the team on Friday. (The restaurant is booked!)
✅ She is flying to Ankara tomorrow morning. (The ticket is bought!)
✅ They aren't playing this weekend. (It's been confirmed — no match.)

Time expressions often used: tomorrow, on Saturday, next week, at 6pm, this evening
💡 Three future forms — quick guide:
I'll open the window → spontaneous NOW · I'm going to the gym → planned intention · I'm meeting Sara at 3pm → confirmed arrangement

✍️ My Weekly Planner

Write 6 sentences about confirmed arrangements you have this week or next week. Use the present continuous for future.

Think about: sport, school, family plans, trips, events, appointments.

🗣️ My Diary

Tell your teacher about your week — as if you are reading from your diary. Use the present continuous for future.

Example opening: "On Monday, I'm playing volleyball after school. On Tuesday, I'm meeting my friend Sara at..."

Speak for at least 1 minute about your real or imagined schedule!
