Presentation

📌 How to Form Comparatives

Adjective typeRuleExample
Short (1 syllable)adj + -ertall → taller
Short ending in -eadj + -rnice → nicer
Short CVC endingdouble consonant + -erbig → bigger
2+ syllablesmore + adjectiveexciting → more exciting
Adjective ending -ychange y → ierhappy → happier
Irregularspecial formgood → better, bad → worse
Structure: Subject + verb + comparative adjective + than + object

✅ Volleyball is more exciting than golf.
✅ My sister is taller than me.
✅ Today's lesson is better than yesterday's.
💡 Remember: We always use than after a comparative when we mention what we are comparing to.
