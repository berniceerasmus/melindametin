🔥 Future Quiz! (5 minutes)

Your teacher will say a situation. You choose the correct future form — quickly!

  • "You see a friend carrying heavy bags. You decide NOW to help." → which form?
  • "You booked a restaurant last week for Saturday." → which form?
  • "You decided last month to start running every day." → which form?
  • "You think your team will probably win." → which form?

📌 Three Future Forms — Summary

FormUseExample
will + base verbSpontaneous decision, prediction, promise, offer"I'll help you." / "I think it will rain."
going to + base verbIntention, plan decided before, prediction with evidence"I'm going to study law." / "Look! It's going to fall!"
Present ContinuousConfirmed arrangement (diary plan)"I'm meeting my coach at 4pm."
💡 All three forms refer to the future — but the reason you choose them is different. Think: Is it NOW? Is it already planned? Is it already in the diary?

⭐ Phase 2 Final Project

Create your own Future Timeline. Write about your plans, intentions, arrangements, and predictions for the future using all three future forms.

Your timeline must include:

  • 3 sentences with will (predictions or promises)
  • 3 sentences with going to (plans and intentions)
  • 3 sentences with Present Continuous (confirmed arrangements)
  • At least 1 negative sentence
  • At least 1 question form
