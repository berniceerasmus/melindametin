📖 City Break — Istanbul vs Ankara

Fatma has visited both Istanbul and Ankara. She thinks Istanbul is the most visited city in Turkey and probably one of the most famous cities in the world. Ankara, the capital, is not as large as Istanbul, but it is more modern in some areas. The food in Istanbul is, in Fatma's opinion, better than in any other Turkish city — especially the seafood. However, Ankara is not as expensive as Istanbul, which makes it a more practical choice for many students. Fatma says the two cities are not as different as people think — both are vibrant, welcoming and full of culture.

1. Istanbul is the most visited city in Turkey, according to Fatma.

2. Ankara is larger than Istanbul.

3. Fatma prefers the seafood in Ankara.

4. Ankara is a cheaper option for students than Istanbul.

5. Fatma has lived in both cities for at least a year.

Choose the correct option.

1. Mount Everest is _______ mountain in the world.

2. This test is not _______ I thought.

3. She works _______ her colleague — they always finish at the same time.

4. Which sentence has a mistake?

Each sentence has ONE mistake. Write the corrected sentence.

1. Istanbul is the more beautiful city in Turkey.

2. He runs more fast than me.

3. This test is not as difficult than I thought.

4. My bag is the more heavy in the class.

5. She is so talented as her sister.

Rewrite each sentence using the word(s) given. Keep the meaning the same.

✍️ Full Comparison Paragraph

Write a paragraph (8–10 sentences) comparing two things you know well — two sports, two cities, two seasons, or two people. Use all three comparison structures at least once each.

  • Superlative: ... is the most/___est ...
