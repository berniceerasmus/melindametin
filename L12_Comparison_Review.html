Phase 2 · Lesson 12

Comparison Review 🔁

Practise comparatives, superlatives, and as…as all together!

🔥 Quiz Yourself! (3 minutes)

Which structure do you use in each situation? Tell your teacher:

  • You compare two things → ___?
  • You compare one thing to all others → ___?
  • You say two things are equal → ___?
  • You say one thing is less than another → ___?

📌 Three Ways to Compare

StructureUseExample
Comparative + thanCompare 2 thingsVolleyball is more popular than tennis.
The + SuperlativeCompare to all othersShe is the best player on the team.
as…as / not as…asEqual / Not equalChess is not as exciting as volleyball.
💡 Remember: comparative + than = comparing 2 · the + superlative = comparing to a group · as…as = same level

Complete with the correct comparison form.

Put the words in the correct order to make a comparison sentence.

1. the / cat / smallest / is / the / team / on / the

2. as / is / this / film / as / not / good / the / book

3. more / was / interesting / the / the / lesson / homework / than

Each sentence has ONE mistake. Find it and write the correct sentence.

1. She is more tall than her sister.

2. This is the most best film I've seen.

3. Chess is not as exciting than volleyball.

✍️ Use All Three Structures!

Write 6 sentences comparing things. You must use:

  • At least 2 comparatives (+ than)
  • At least 2 superlatives (the + -est / most)
  • At least 2 as…as or not as…as

🗣️ Mini Debate

Choose a topic and make an argument to your teacher using comparisons:

  • Volleyball vs. basketball — which is better?
  • Living in a city vs. countryside
  • Watching sport vs. playing sport

Use at least 5 comparison structures. Mix all three types!
