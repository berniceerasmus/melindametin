Grammar — The Simple Past Rule

Learn the most important rule for talking about the past

8 min ▼

🕐 What is the Simple Past?

We use the Simple Past to talk about things that already happened — yesterday, last week, last year, or any time in the past that is finished.

🇹🇷 Türkçe'deki "-dı/-di/-du/-dü" ekine karşılık gelir. Geçmişte olmuş ve bitmiş eylemler için kullanılır. Örn: "Dün izledim" → "I watched yesterday."

✅ The Golden Rule — Just Add -ed!

For regular verbs, making the Simple Past is easy — you just add -ed to the end of the verb!

BASE VERB  +  -ed  =  SIMPLE PAST
watch + ed = watched  ·  play + ed = played  ·  walk + ed = walked
cook + ed = cooked  ·  clean + ed = cleaned  ·  listen + ed = listened
🇹🇷 Bu kural düzenli fiiller (regular verbs) için geçerlidir. Sadece sonuna -ed ekle! Kolay, değil mi? 😊

⚠️ One Special Rule for Lesson 1

If the verb already ends in the letter -e, just add -d (not -ed — that would give you double -e!)

live + d = lived  ·  love + d = loved  ·  arrive + d = arrived  ·  use + d = used
✗ "liveed" · "loveed" — WRONG! Don't double the -e.
🇹🇷 Fiil zaten -e ile bitiyorsa sadece -d ekle. "live" → "lived" (lived değil liveed!)

📌 Important: Simple Past Does NOT Change!

Unlike present tense (he plays, she watches), the Simple Past form is the same for every person. No -s at the end!

I watched · You watched · He watched · She watched · We watched · They watched
✗ "She watcheds" · "He playeds" — WRONG! No -s in the past!

Common mistakes to remember:
• watch ✗ → watched ✓ (always add -ed for past!)
• enjoied ✗ → enjoyed ✓ (enjoy ends in -y — just add -ed!)
• traveleds ✗ → travelled ✓ (never add -s to past tense!)
• walkd ✗ → walked ✓ (walk doesn't end in -e, so it's -ed not -d)
• plays ✗ → played ✓ (past tense, not present!)
