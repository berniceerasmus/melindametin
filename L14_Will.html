Phase 2 · Lesson 14

will ⚡

Learn how to talk about spontaneous decisions, predictions, and promises.

Warm-Up

🔥 Predict the Future! (3 minutes)

Make a prediction about the future. Start with "I think..."

  • What will the weather be like tomorrow?
  • What will you do after school this year?
  • Who will win the next World Cup?

Try to use "will" in your answers — even if you're not sure yet!

Presentation

📌 Structure — will

FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + will + base verbI will help you with that.
NegativeSubject + will not / won't + base verbShe won't be late.
QuestionWill + subject + base verb?Will you come to the game?
Short answerYes, I will. / No, I won't.—
When do we use will?

✅ Spontaneous decision (decided NOW): "The phone is ringing — I'll answer it!"
✅ Prediction (opinion about future): "I think Turkey will win the match."
✅ Promise / offer: "I will help you study for the test."

Contraction: I will → I'll · He will → He'll · Will not → Won't
💡 will vs going to: Use will for decisions made at the moment of speaking. Use going to for plans already decided before.

✍️ Promises & Predictions

Write 6 sentences using will / won't. Try to use different functions (prediction, promise, offer, spontaneous decision).

🗣️ Future Predictions

Tell your teacher your predictions about the future. Use "I think... will..."

  • What will life be like in 20 years?
  • What will you be doing in 10 years?
  • Will technology change sport in the future?

Speak for at least 1 minute. Use will and won't both!
