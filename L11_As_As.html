Phase 2 · Lesson 11

as…as / not as…as ⚖️

Learn how to say two things are equal — or not equal!

Warm-Up

🔥 Same or Different? (3 minutes)

Look at the pairs. Are they the same, or is one bigger/better?

  • A cat and a lion — are they equally dangerous?
  • Turkish and English — are they equally difficult?
  • Basketball and volleyball — are they equally popular in Turkey?

Discuss with your teacher. We will learn the grammar to express these ideas!

Presentation

📌 as…as and not as…as

StructureMeaningExample
as + adj + asThey are equalShe is as tall as her sister.
not as + adj + asOne is less than the otherChess is not as exciting as volleyball.
✅ My bag is as heavy as yours. (= same weight)
✅ The film was not as good as the book. (= the book was better)
✅ He is as fast as the best player on the team.
✅ This coffee is not as hot as I expected.
💡 Tip: Not as…as and a comparative often mean the same thing:
"Chess is not as exciting as volleyball" = "Volleyball is more exciting than chess."
