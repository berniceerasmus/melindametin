📖 Read the text carefully

Emma and her brother Luca are twins. They look quite similar, but their personalities are very different. Emma is not as quiet as Luca — she loves chatting with friends and is nearly as sociable as their mother. Luca, on the other hand, prefers books and music. He is just as intelligent as Emma, but he is not as outgoing as she is. In sport, Emma is faster than Luca, but Luca is as strong as Emma. Their parents always say the two children are as different as night and day — but just as loveable!

Are these statements True (T), False (F), or Not Given (NG)?

1. Emma and Luca are twin brother and sister.

2. Luca is more sociable than their mother.

3. Luca and Emma have the same level of intelligence.

4. Emma gets better exam results than Luca.

5. In sport, Luca is as fast as Emma.

6. Their parents are proud of both children.

Choose the best answer (a, b or c) for each sentence.

1. My phone is _______ yours — it cost much less.

2. She runs _______ an Olympic athlete. She's incredible!

3. The new café is _______ the old one. They both have great coffee.

4. Which sentence is CORRECT?

Rewrite using not as…as or as…as so the meaning stays the same. Keep all the information.

1. Football is more popular than volleyball in Turkey. → Volleyball is...

2. Winter is colder than autumn. → Autumn is...

3. My brother is the same height as me. → I am...

4. This homework is more difficult than yesterday's. → Yesterday's homework...

5. The black cat weighs the same as the white cat. → The white cat...

Each sentence has ONE mistake. Find it and write the correct sentence.

1. He is not as tall than his brother.

2. This bag is as heavier as that one.

3. She is not as quickly as her sister.

4. My dog is so friendly as yours.

5. This coffee is not as hotter as the last cup.
