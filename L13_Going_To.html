going to 🔮

Learn how to talk about plans and intentions for the future.

🔥 Your Weekend (3 minutes)

Tell your teacher about your plans. Use these questions as a guide:

  • What are you going to do this weekend?
  • Are you going to play volleyball?
  • Are you going to study English?

Don't worry about being perfect — just try! We will study the grammar now.

📌 Structure — going to

FormStructureExample
Affirmativeam/is/are + going to + verbI am going to play volleyball tomorrow.
Negativeam/is/are + not + going to + verbShe is not going to watch the match.
QuestionAm/Is/Are + subject + going to + verb?Are you going to study tonight?
When do we use going to?

✅ Plans & intentions: I am going to start a new art class next month.
✅ Predictions with evidence: Look at those clouds — it is going to rain!

Contractions: I'm going to... / She's going to... / They're going to...
💡 Tip: "Going to" is for decisions you made before the moment of speaking. It's already planned!

✍️ Your Future Plans

Write 6 sentences about your real plans for the next week, month, or year. Use going to in each sentence.

Use a mix of affirmative, negative, and question forms if you can!

🗣️ Interview!

Your teacher will ask you questions about your future plans. Answer using going to.

Example questions:

  • What are you going to do after school today?
  • Where are you going to go on your next holiday?
  • What are you going to study in the future?
